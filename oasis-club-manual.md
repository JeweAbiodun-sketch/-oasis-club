# Oasis Club App — User Manual

This manual is written one role at a time. Each section only covers what
that role can actually do — if something isn't mentioned, that role
doesn't have access to it in the app.

A quick note on how access works in this app: most administrative powers
(editing members, dues, minutes, finances, the constitution) belong to
whoever is logged in with one of the three **officer PINs** — Secretary,
Treasurer, or Financial Secretary — not to whoever holds a title like
President or Vice-President in real life. Holding an officer title in the
club's records gives you a few specific things (described below for each
role) but does not automatically unlock those officer PINs. If your role
needs one of those PINs for club business, the current Secretary,
Treasurer, or Financial Secretary will need to share it with you, or the
club may choose to have you take over that office and its PIN directly.

## 1. President

### Signing in
1. Open the app. On the welcome screen, tap **"I already have a PIN"**
   (or **"Set up my access"** if this is your very first time).
2. Select your name from the list.
3. Enter your personal 4-digit PIN.

If this is your first time, you'll instead see a **"Welcome, [your first
name]"** screen asking you to create a 4-digit PIN. Pick one and confirm
it — from then on, that's the PIN you'll use to sign in.

Your PIN is personal to you as a member, not a special "President PIN."
Nobody else automatically knows it, and it's separate from the Secretary,
Treasurer, and Financial Secretary officer PINs mentioned above.

### What you can see
Once signed in, your sidebar shows:

- **Overview** — the club's dashboard: dues collected, account balance,
  upcoming events, birthdays, recent activity.
- **My Profile** — your own details, photo, PIN, dues status, attendance
  record, fines (if any), and your membership ID card.
- **Members** — the full membership list.
- **Photos** — the club's shared photo gallery.
- **Dues & Subscriptions** — who's paid, who hasn't, and a button to
  submit your own proof of payment.
- **Meeting Minutes** — past meeting records.
- **Project Board** — a simple task board for tracking club work
  (Building Project, Events, Admin, Welfare, General).
- **Financial Year** — income/expenditure and the year's financial summary.
- **Committee & Officers** — the list of officers and quorum rules.
- **Constitution** — the club's constitution document.
- **Upcoming Events** — events the club has scheduled.
- **News & Notices** — club announcements and member posts (see below —
  this is where your role matters most).
- **Polls & Voting** — any open club polls.
- **Welfare Contributions**, **Pledges** — welfare fund and pledge records.
- **Committees** — only visible if you're signed in with an officer PIN,
  not with your personal President PIN.
- **Projects** — community/building project tracking.

Two pages — **Welfare Loans** and **Payment Review** — only appear for
the Welfare Committee, Treasurer, or Financial Secretary, so you won't see
them unless you also hold one of those positions.

### What's specific to you as President

**Reviewing News & Notices.** This is the one place in the app where the
President role itself (not an officer PIN) gives you a real power: you
and the PRO are the only two who can approve or reject posts that members
submit for the notice board.

- Open **News & Notices**. If there's anything waiting, you'll see an
  **"Awaiting your review"** section at the top, separate from the
  published notices below it.
- Each pending post shows who submitted it and what they wrote.
- Tap **Approve** to publish it immediately to the whole club, or tap
  **Reject** to decline it — you can optionally add a short note
  explaining why, which the member will see on their own post.
- You can also write and post a notice directly yourself, the same way
  any member can — tap **"Write a post"** at the top of the page. Posts
  you write still need approval from you or the PRO before anyone else
  sees them, same as everyone else's.

**Your ID card.** On the **Member ID Card** card in your profile, your
card will always show **M001** as your Member ID, regardless of when you
joined the club — the President's seat is permanently numbered first.

**Committee & Officers page.** You'll see your name listed first under
"President" in the officer list shown there. Whoever holds an officer
PIN can reassign any office — including yours — from this page; see the
Secretary section of this manual for how that works.

### What you can't do as President
Unless you're also separately logged in with the Secretary, Treasurer, or
Financial Secretary PIN, you **cannot**:
- Edit, add, or remove members
- Edit dues amounts or mark a member's dues as paid
- Add, edit, or delete meeting minutes
- Add, edit, or delete financial transactions
- Edit the constitution or upload a new copy
- Approve or reject payment evidence submitted by members
- Approve welfare loans (that's the Welfare Committee's job, plus the
  Treasurer/Financial Secretary for releasing funds)

If you need any of these done, ask whichever officer currently holds the
relevant PIN, or raise it with the club to have the right person handle
it.

## 2. Secretary

### Signing in
On the welcome screen, choose **"I already have a PIN"**, then select
**your name from the officer login list** and enter the Secretary PIN
(this is a shared club PIN, not your personal one — ask whoever currently
holds it if you don't have it). Signing in this way gives you full
officer access across the app.

You can also sign in with your own personal member PIN if you have a
member record. That gets you the same access as any regular member, plus
two small extras described under "Secretary-only powers" below — but it
does **not** unlock the editing tools described in the next section. For
day-to-day Secretary work (adding members, logging minutes, managing
dues, and so on) you need the officer PIN.

### What officer-PIN access unlocks
Everything below is shared equally by whoever is logged in with the
Secretary, Treasurer, or Financial Secretary PIN — it isn't exclusive to
you, but it's the core of the Secretary's day-to-day work in the app:

**Members**
- **Add member** — name, role, status, phone, email, join date, photo,
  and a starting PIN for them.
- **Edit member** — same fields, on an existing record.
- Setting a member's status to **Suspended** or **Dismissed** instantly
  blocks their app login; setting it back to **Active** restores it.
  Suspended/dismissed members move to a separate **Archive** tab, where
  you can **Restore** them back to active at any time.
- **Permanently remove** a member record entirely (separate from
  suspending them) — the app will ask you to confirm, since this cannot
  be undone.
- Edit a member's PIN directly from the same form if they're locked out.

**Meeting Minutes**
- **Log minutes** — meeting title, date, type (Monthly meeting, Annual
  General Meeting, Special General Meeting, or Committee meeting), and
  free-text notes on what was discussed and decided.
- Mark **attendance** on any logged meeting — tap the attendance button
  on that meeting's row to select which active members were present.
  This feeds each member's attendance record on their own profile.
- Edit or delete an existing minutes entry.

**Dues & Subscriptions**
- Manually edit any member's recorded dues-paid amount.
- Set the annual due amount and the year label shown across the app.

**Financial Year**
- Add, edit, or delete income/expenditure transactions.

**Overview (dashboard)**
- Set the next AGM date, shown under "Meeting rhythm" on the dashboard
  everyone sees when they sign in.

**Committee & Officers**
- This page shows who currently holds each office (President,
  Vice-President, Secretary, Assistant Secretary, Treasurer, Financial
  Secretary, PRO), with quorum figures calculated live from the active
  membership — you don't set quorum numbers directly.
- Tap **Assign** (or **Reassign** if the office is already filled) on any
  office, including your own, to put a different active member into that
  role. You can either pick an existing member from the list, or type a
  name directly for someone not yet on the roster. This is how someone
  becomes "President" or any other office in this app's records — it's a
  field on their member profile, not a separate login.

**Committees**
- Create or delete committees, and add or remove members from them
  (including setting a committee's chairperson).

**Events, Polls, Pledges**
- Add or delete events, create or delete polls, and record or delete
  pledges.

**News & Notices, Payment Evidence submissions**
- Remove any post or submission, even ones you didn't submit yourself
  (regular members can only withdraw their own pending ones).

**Constitution**
- Upload or replace the official constitution PDF — this updates the
  download link everyone sees.

### Secretary-only powers
A few things are restricted to the Secretary specifically — these work
whether you're signed in with the officer PIN, or with your own personal
PIN as a member whose role is set to "Secretary":

- **Assistant source text.** On the Constitution page, a "Secretary
  only" panel lets you edit the text the club's AI assistant uses to
  answer member questions. Saving updates it for everyone.
- **AI assistant key.** Tap the gear icon on the assistant panel to add
  or remove the API key that powers full AI answers for the whole club.
  Everyone can see whether AI is currently active; only you can change
  the key itself. Choices include Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI (ChatGPT),
  Cohere, or Google Gemini.

### What you cannot do, even as Secretary
A few things are reserved for other officers specifically:
- **Approving or rejecting payment evidence** is Treasurer/Financial
  Secretary only (you can still delete a submission, but not approve it).
- **Releasing welfare funds** is Treasurer/Financial Secretary only.
- **Approving welfare loans** belongs to the Welfare Committee.
- **Reviewing News & Notices submissions** belongs to the President and
  PRO, not the Secretary.

## 3. Treasurer

### Signing in
On the welcome screen, choose **"I already have a PIN"**, then select
your name from the officer login list and enter the Treasurer PIN (a
shared club PIN — ask whoever currently holds it if you don't have it).

Unlike the Secretary, there's no fallback here: signing in with your own
personal member PIN — even if your profile role is set to "Treasurer" —
does **not** unlock any of the Treasurer powers below. Every one of them
requires the actual Treasurer PIN login. This was a deliberate choice so
that nobody can release club funds or approve payments just because their
profile says "Treasurer" — only the real PIN does that.

### Things you share with the Financial Secretary
A few powers belong equally to whoever is logged in as Treasurer or
Financial Secretary — neither of you needs the other to act:

- **Payment Review** (a sidebar page only the two of you and the Welfare
  Committee can see). Members upload proof of payment from their own
  profile; this page lists everything waiting on you. You can **Reject**
  a submission with an optional note explaining why.
- **Releasing welfare funds.** On the Welfare Loans page, tap **Release
  fund** to hand money over to the Welfare Committee's available balance,
  with an amount, date, and short note.
- Seeing each other's **last login time** on the Members page, alongside
  the Secretary.

### Things only you can do as Treasurer
A few things are genuinely Treasurer-only — the Financial Secretary
cannot do these, even logged in with their own officer PIN:

- **Approving** a payment submission on the Payment Review page. The
  Financial Secretary can reject a submission, but only you can approve
  one — approving is what actually updates the member's dues record or
  adds the building-project contribution to the club's finances.
- **Dues spreadsheet** (on the Dues & Subscriptions page) — a "Download
  dues CSV" / "Upload updated CSV" pair for bulk-updating who's paid what.
  The file lists every active member with their Member ID, name, role,
  status, annual due, amount paid, and outstanding balance. Edit only the
  **Amount Paid** column and re-upload — the app matches rows by Member
  ID and **overwrites** that member's dues-paid total with whatever the
  file says (it does not add to the existing figure).
- **Transactions spreadsheet** (on the Financial Year page) — the same
  download/upload pattern, but for the income/expenditure ledger.
- **Sync everything from Google Sheets**, also on the Financial Year
  page. This is a live link to one specific shared spreadsheet (you'll
  see the link right there on the page) with four tabs: TRANSACTIONS,
  WELFARE, PLEDGES, and ANNUAL DUES. One tap pulls in anything new from
  all four tabs at once, and tells you how many new entries it found in
  each. A few things worth knowing about how it behaves:
  - It never duplicates a row it's already imported.
  - For the sheet to be readable at all, it must be **published to the
    web** from Google Sheets (File → Share → Publish to web) — if it
    isn't, the sync will fail with a "make sure the sheet is published"
    message.
  - Each tab needs a recognizable header row: a column ending in "DATE"
    plus "DESCRIPTION" for Transactions; a date column plus a name column
    for Welfare and Pledges; a column ending in "MEMBER" plus a column
    containing "AMT PAID" or "AMOUNT PAID" for Annual Dues.
  - **Annual Dues works differently from the other three.** Transactions
    and Pledges *add* new rows as new records. Annual Dues instead
    **overwrites** each matched member's dues-paid total to match the
    sheet, the same way the CSV upload does — it's matched by member
    name in the sheet, so names need to match your member records
    exactly for this to find the right person.
  - **Welfare entries synced this way only land on the device you synced
    from.** Welfare & Levies isn't part of the app's regular club-wide
    sync — unlike Transactions, Pledges, and Annual Dues, which update
    for everyone immediately.

### Things any officer PIN can do
Like the Secretary, signing in as Treasurer also gives you the shared
officer powers described in the Secretary section above: managing
members, logging minutes, editing the constitution, managing committees,
events, polls, and pledges, and removing others' news posts or payment
submissions. The Secretary-only items (the AI assistant key and its
source text) remain Secretary-only even for you.

### What you cannot do as Treasurer
- **Approving welfare loans** is the Welfare Committee's decision, not
  yours — your part is releasing the money once a loan is active, not
  approving the loan itself.
- The **AI assistant key and source text** are Secretary-only.

## 4. Financial Secretary

### Signing in
On the welcome screen, choose **"I already have a PIN"**, then select
your name from the officer login list and enter the Financial Secretary
PIN (a shared club PIN — ask whoever currently holds it if you don't
have it).

As with the Treasurer, there's no personal-PIN fallback for this role:
signing in with your own member PIN, even if your profile role says
"Financial Secretary," does not unlock anything below. Only the real
Financial Secretary PIN does.

### What you can do
Everywhere this role appears in the app, it appears paired with the
Treasurer — there's nothing reserved for you alone, but you share fully
in:

- **Payment Review.** You'll see this page in the sidebar, listing every
  payment submission waiting on review. You can **Reject** a submission
  with an optional note. Approving is Treasurer-only — if a submission
  looks right to you, let the Treasurer know so they can approve it; the
  page itself will remind you of this with a note in the page header.
- **Releasing welfare funds.** On the Welfare Loans page, you can tap
  **Release fund** to hand money over to the Welfare Committee, the same
  as the Treasurer can.
- A pending-count badge appears on the Payment Review sidebar link, and
  a toast notification when a new submission arrives, the same as for
  the Treasurer.
- Seeing **last login times** on the Members page, alongside the
  Secretary and Treasurer.

### Things any officer PIN can do
Signing in with the Financial Secretary PIN also gives you the shared
officer powers described in the Secretary section: managing members,
logging minutes, editing the constitution, managing committees, events,
polls, and pledges, and removing others' news posts or payment
submissions.

### What you cannot do as Financial Secretary
- **Approving** a payment submission — only the Treasurer can do this.
  You can reject one, but the final yes is theirs.
- The **Dues spreadsheet** and **Transactions spreadsheet** CSV tools,
  and the **"Sync all sheets from Google Sheets"** button, are all
  Treasurer-only, even though you can see the same Dues and Financial
  Year pages they're on.
- **Approving welfare loans** belongs to the Welfare Committee.
- The **AI assistant key and source text** are Secretary-only.

## 5. PRO (Public Relations Officer)

### Signing in
There's no separate PRO PIN. Sign in the normal member way: tap **"I
already have a PIN,"** select your name, and enter your personal PIN.

### What you can see
The same full set of pages described under the President's "What you can
see" section above — every regular member sees the same sidebar, with
**Welfare Loans**, **Payment Review**, and **Committees** hidden unless
you separately hold one of those positions or an officer PIN.

### What's specific to you as PRO
**Reviewing News & Notices.** This is your main power in the app, shared
only with the President — between the two of you, someone has to approve
every post before it reaches the rest of the club.

- Open **News & Notices**. Anything waiting for review shows in an
  **"Awaiting your review"** section at the top.
- Tap **Approve** to publish a post immediately, or **Reject** with an
  optional note the member will see on their own post.
- You can write and post notices yourself too, the same as any member —
  tap **"Write a post."** Your own posts still need approval from you or
  the President before they're visible to anyone else.

Unlike the President, your ID card doesn't get a fixed number — it's
numbered the same way as any other member's, based on join date.

### What you can't do as PRO
The same restrictions as a regular member apply — see "What you can't do
as President" above for the full list (editing members, dues, minutes,
finances, the constitution, approving payments, or welfare loans). None
of those are unlocked by the PRO role; they all need an officer PIN.

## 6. Welfare Committee

### Signing in
There's no separate Welfare Committee PIN. Sign in the normal member
way: tap **"I already have a PIN,"** select your name, and enter your
personal PIN.

### How you get on this committee
An officer (signed in with the Secretary, Treasurer, or Financial
Secretary PIN) adds you from the **Committees** page — a committee
called "Welfare Committee" with your name added to it. If you don't see
the things described below, check with an officer that you've actually
been added there.

### What's specific to you on this committee
Once you're a member of the Welfare Committee, a **Welfare Loans** page
appears in your sidebar (also visible to the Treasurer and Financial
Secretary). On it, you can:

- **Propose a loan** for any active member — pick who it's for, the
  principal amount, an interest rate (0% is fine), the purpose, and an
  optional due date.
- **Approve a loan someone else proposed.** Every proposed loan needs
  sign-off from a *second*, different committee member before it
  becomes active — you can't approve your own proposal, and the same
  person can't give both approvals on one loan. The app enforces this
  automatically; the **Approve** button simply won't appear if you've
  already approved it once, or if you're the one who proposed it.
- **Log a welfare expense** that isn't a loan — money spent from the
  welfare purse, with an amount, date, and description (for example,
  something reported and agreed at a monthly meeting).
- **Record a repayment** against any active loan, with an amount
  (pre-filled with the outstanding balance) and the date it was paid.
  Once a loan is fully repaid, its status changes to "Repaid in full."
- See the welfare purse's running totals: how much the Treasurer or
  Financial Secretary has released into it, how much is out in active
  loans and logged expenses, and what's currently available for new
  loans or expenses.

Releasing money *into* the welfare purse in the first place is done by
the Treasurer or Financial Secretary, not by the committee — see their
sections above for the **Release fund** button.

### What you can't do
- You can't release funds into the welfare purse yourself.
- Outside of the Welfare Loans page, your access is the same as any
  regular member — committee membership doesn't unlock anything else in
  the app (editing members, dues, minutes, finances, and so on still
  need an officer PIN).

## 7. Member

This section covers everyone — every other role in this manual builds on
top of what's described here. If you hold a title like President, PRO,
or a seat on the Welfare Committee, this is also your baseline access
whenever you're not specifically using one of the powers described in
your own section above.

### Signing in
1. On the welcome screen, tap **"I already have a PIN"** if you've used
   the app before, or **"Set up my access"** the first time.
2. Select your name from the list and enter your PIN.

First time signing in, you'll see a **"Welcome, [your first name]"**
screen instead, asking you to create a 4-digit PIN. That becomes your
sign-in PIN from then on. Nobody else automatically knows it.

### Your sidebar
- **Overview** — the club dashboard: dues collected, account balance,
  upcoming events, birthdays, recent activity.
- **My Profile** — everything about your own record, covered in detail
  below.
- **Members** — the membership list (name, role, joined date, contact,
  status — officers see a few extra columns you won't).
- **Photos** — a gallery of every member's profile photo. Tap anyone to
  open their record.
- **Dues & Subscriptions** — see who's paid and who hasn't, and submit
  your own proof of payment.
- **Meeting Minutes** — read past meeting records.
- **Project Board** — a shared task board (Building Project, Events,
  Admin, Welfare, General columns) that any member can add to.
- **Financial Year** — the club's income/expenditure summary.
- **Committee & Officers** — who currently holds each office.
- **Constitution** — the club's constitution document.
- **Upcoming Events** — events the club has scheduled.
- **News & Notices** — read approved notices, and submit your own.
- **Polls & Voting** — vote in any open poll.
- **Welfare Contributions**, **Pledges** — view-only records; officers
  add and edit entries here.
- **Projects** — view-only community/building project tracking.

**Welfare Loans**, **Payment Review**, and **Committees** don't appear
in your sidebar at all unless you separately hold a position that
unlocks them (see the other sections of this manual).

### My Profile — your own self-service page
This is the one page where you can change things yourself, without
needing anyone's help:

- **Engagement score.** A bar showing how active you've been — based on
  meeting attendance plus things you've done in the app (notices
  posted, events created, polls voted in, payments reported).
- **Online status toggle.** Switch on whether you show as online to club
  officers.
- **Photo.** Upload a JPG or PNG under 2MB, or remove it.
- **Your details.** Edit your name, phone, and email. Your role/office
  and join date are shown but locked — only an officer can change those.
- **Change my PIN.** You'll need your current PIN to set a new one.
- **Financial standing.** Your annual due, what you've paid, and your
  outstanding balance for the current year, with a clear paid-up or
  outstanding message.
- **Date of birth.** Optional — saving it adds you to the birthday board
  on the Overview dashboard. You can clear it any time.
- **Attendance record.** Your meeting attendance percentage, once any
  meetings have recorded attendance.
- **Fines.** Only shown if you have any on record — lists the reason,
  date, and amount for each. Only an officer can waive or remove one.
- **Submit payment evidence.** Tap **"Upload proof of payment"** to send
  a screenshot or photo of a bank transfer, receipt, or alert (JPG, PNG,
  or PDF, under 10MB) to the Treasurer and Financial Secretary, marking
  whether it's for your annual dues or a Building Project contribution.
  You'll see each submission's status here — pending, approved, or
  rejected (with a note if it was rejected) — and you can withdraw any
  submission that's still pending if you change your mind. Once
  something is approved, it can't be withdrawn or edited.
- **Member ID Card.** Tap **"View ID Card"** to generate a printable
  identity card with your name, role, and membership details, with a
  print/save-as-PDF option.

### News & Notices — submitting your own post
- Tap **"Write a post"** at the top of the page.
- Give it a title and write your message using the formatting toolbar
  (bold, italic, underline, bullet or numbered lists, text colour), and
  optionally attach a file.
- Tap **"Submit for review."** Your post goes to the President and PRO —
  it only appears on the public notice board once one of them approves
  it. You'll see your own pending and rejected posts on the page (with
  any rejection note), even though other members can't see them yet.
  You can remove your own post yourself as long as it hasn't been
  approved; once it's published, removing it is up to an officer.

### Polls & Voting
- Tap an option to cast your vote — you get exactly one vote per poll,
  and the app won't let you change it afterwards.
- You won't see how the voting currently stands until after you've
  voted, or until the poll closes — this is deliberate, so the running
  results don't influence your choice beforehand.

### Project Board
- Tap **"New task"** to add a task in any category, optionally assigning
  it to an active member and adding notes.
- Tap a task to edit it, and use the move buttons on each card to shift
  it between To Do, In Progress, and Done.
- Use the category filter pills at the top to focus on one area at a
  time.

### What you can't do as a Member
Everything described in the officer sections of this manual — adding or
editing members, dues, minutes, finances, the constitution, approving
payments or welfare loans, and managing committees — needs an officer
PIN, or in a few specific cases, the President/PRO/Welfare Committee
positions described earlier. If you need any of that done, the easiest
path is usually to ask whichever officer currently handles it.
